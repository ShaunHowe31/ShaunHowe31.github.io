Eckington Air Quality Analysis


This is a website dedicated to air quality analysis for 4 PurpleAir air pollution sensors located in the Eckington Neighborhood in Washington, DC. The PurpleAir sensors are low-cost pollution monitoring devices. Because these are low-cost sensors, it is necessary to correct the data coming from the sensors. The data used in the analysis below were corrected based on the methodology developed by scientists at the Environmental Protection Agency (EPA) (Barkjohn et al 2021). After the data are corrected, they are aggregated into hourly and daily averages. Figures labeled "Highest Confidence" mean that a threshold of 90% was required for an average to be taken. This means that for an hourly average, 54 minutes of data were required to create an hourly average and for a daily average 21.6 hours of data are reuiqred to create a daily average.

Air Quality Averages

Daily and yearly averages as the typical standards set by the EPA as defined by the National Ambient Air Quality Standards (NAAQS).

  • Daily Averaged PM2.5 (Barkjohn Correction) - The same data as above except a 90% completness threshold was used to create the daily average.
  • Hourly PM2.5 Climatology (Barkjohn Correction) - Hourly aggregated climatology of PM2.5 using 90% confidence filtering. These plots show how PM2.5 values increase around rush hour.

Air Quality Standard Exceedances

Hourly air quality analysis shows the short-term fluctuation in air pollution compared to the daily EPA standard.

  • WHO PM2.5 Standard Hourly Exceedances (Barkjohn Correction) - The daily sum of hours exceeding the World Health Organization (WHO) PM2.5 stanard of 25 µg/m³.
  • EPA PM2.5 Standard Hourly Exceedances (Barkjohn Correction) - The daily sum of hours exceeding the EPA PM2.5 stanard of 35 µg/m³.
